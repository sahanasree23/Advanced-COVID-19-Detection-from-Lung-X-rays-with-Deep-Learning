COVID-19, caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, emerged in late 2019 and rapidly escalated into a global pandemic. Characterized by symptoms such as fever, cough, and shortness of breath, the disease spreads primarily through respiratory droplets. Preventive measures like wearing masks, physical distancing, and vaccination have been crucial in managing the pandemic. While vaccines have significantly reduced severe cases and transmission, the virus continues to impact healthcare systems, economies, and daily life worldwide. Ongoing efforts focus on vaccination, treatment, and public health strategies to control the spread and mitigate the effects of COVID-19.
